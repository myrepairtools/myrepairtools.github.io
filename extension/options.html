What's new in v2.5.78
🧾 Quote Builder — a "＋ Quote" button on every MobileSentrix/cpr.parts tile and a floating cart that totals a multi-part repair in real time (priciest part = the Repair, rest = Add-ons; 📋 copies it). ⚙ Status Override — an always-available "Force status" dropdown to reopen or re-status a ticket after RepairQ hides its own control (off by default). 📣 Follow-Up capture now pops the moment you add the customer at check-in — your choice is saved onto the ticket the instant it's created.

Version 2.5.78
  • 🧾 Quote Builder cart. On MobileSentrix & cpr.parts, each product tile gets a "＋ Quote" button and picked parts collect into a floating cart (top-right, follows the page). It totals what we'd charge for a multi-part repair in real time — the priciest part is billed as the Repair (part + $100 labor), the rest as Add-ons, using your Franchise/CAP pricing model. Re-pick the Repair line with ☆, 📋 copies the quote, and the cart survives moving between product pages. Toggle under the new MobileSentrix Tools card.
  • 🗂️ MobileSentrix Tools got its own Options card (Stock Badges, Price Overlay, Quote Builder + the pricing-model setting), split out of Workflow tools.
  • ⚙ Status Override. When RepairQ removes a ticket's status control — a closed ticket, or after the business day rolls over — a "Force status" dropdown + Apply is injected onto the ticket so you can reopen or re-status it anyway (writes straight to RepairQ). Off by default under Workflow tools; it forces status changes RepairQ normally restricts.
  • 📣 Follow-Up capture pops at customer-add. The "how does the customer want to hear back?" modal now opens the instant you add the customer on the check-in/create screen — no ticket number exists yet, so your choice is stashed and written onto the ticket (Supabase + a ticket note) the moment it's saved.
Version 2.5.77
  • 🛠️ Parts Gate default OFF. It was blocking ticket close when a repair had no bundled part — but plenty of repairs (data transfers, diagnostics, unlocks) legitimately have no part, and there are other backup checks. Now off by default; toggle it on per-store in Options → RepairQ workflow tools.
  • 🐛 Follow-Up modal fixed on new tickets. The "how does the customer want to hear back?" modal stopped auto-popping after check-in because it relied on the create-page URL, which varies by RepairQ check-in flow. It now also triggers on any ticket showing status "New," so it reliably pops once right after a ticket is created.
Version 2.5.76
  • 💬 Incoming-text alerts made reliable. The background check that powers the unread badge and new-text desktop notifications used to stop running if the store wasn't detected the instant the page loaded (it only ran after you opened the panel). It now re-reads the store every cycle, so alerts fire for new customer texts without opening Message Center first. The open inbox also live-refreshes when a text arrives instead of staying stale until reopened.
Version 2.5.75
  • 🐛 "Note cannot be blank" on Ready for Pickup — fixed. Your store requires a note when a ticket goes to Ready for Pickup, so RepairQ pops its "Add Note" box. Our button re-fire was leaving it empty → blank note + error + the status wouldn't change. Now, when that box appears, the extension fills it with the ready-for-pickup note ("📣 text sent to …") and submits it, so the status change completes and the note is meaningful instead of blank. Verified our own note-writing wasn't the cause — this was RepairQ's required-note prompt.
Version 2.5.74
