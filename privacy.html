myRepairTools

Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 14, 2026

myRepairTools is the internal tool suite built for CPR Oregon staff: the web app at myrepairtools.com and a companion Chrome extension that adds repair-workflow, customer-messaging, inventory, and labeling tools inside the RepairQ point-of-sale web app (cpr.repairq.io). It is intended only for authorized CPR Oregon employees.

What the extension accesses

  • RepairQ page content — while you use RepairQ, the extension reads the current page (ticket number, status, line items, store, the signed-in user, and the customer contact details already shown on the ticket) so its tools can act on that ticket.
  • Supplier pages — on MobileSentrix and cpr.parts it reads part numbers on the page to show pricing and on-hand stock.
  • Your settings — feature on/off toggles are saved in Chrome's extension storage.

What it sends, and to whom

Actions you take are sent to CPR Oregon's own backend (a Supabase project) and to the services CPR Oregon uses to run the business:

  • Customer messaging — when a customer is texted or called from a ticket, the phone number and message content are sent through CPR Oregon's RingCentral and Twilio accounts and logged.
  • Repair & inventory data — screen-buyback records, blacklist checks, follow-up preferences, and similar tool data are stored in CPR Oregon's database.
  • AI assistant — questions you type into the in-app assistant are processed by CPR Oregon's backend (which uses the Anthropic API) to return answers.
  • Issue reports — if you use "Report Issue," your description plus the page URL, ticket number, store, your name, and the extension version are logged so the problem can be fixed.

QuickBooks Online integration

With the business owner's explicit authorization (Intuit's OAuth consent screen), myRepairTools connects to CPR Oregon's own QuickBooks Online company to support month-end bookkeeping. Through this connection it:

  • Reads the chart of accounts (account names and IDs) so the owner can map each store to its Cash on Hand and revenue accounts.
  • Creates journal entries that the owner individually reviews and approves before each posting — nothing is posted automatically.

OAuth tokens for this connection are stored encrypted in CPR Oregon's backend, are never placed in the browser, and are used solely for the functions above. QuickBooks data is not sold, shared with third parties, or used for any purpose other than CPR Oregon's own bookkeeping. The connection can be revoked at any time from myRepairTools settings or from Intuit's connected-apps page, which invalidates the stored tokens.

Authentication

The extension uses your existing RepairQ browser session and a shared, restricted access key to reach CPR Oregon's backend. It does not ask for, store, or transmit your passwords. Sensitive integration credentials (RingCentral, Twilio, Square, Intuit/QuickBooks, the AI API key) are held server-side and are never placed in the browser.

How the information is used

Only to provide the extension's features for CPR Oregon's repair operations — contacting customers about their repairs, tracking parts and inventory, and improving the tools. We do not sell or rent any data, do not use it for advertising, and do not use it to determine creditworthiness or for lending. Data is not shared with third parties except the service providers named above that are required to deliver these features.

Retention

Records are kept in CPR Oregon's systems for as long as needed to run the business and meet recordkeeping needs. Per-visit customer follow-up preferences are removed when the related ticket is closed.
